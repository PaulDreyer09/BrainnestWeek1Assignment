Planche style Calisthenics exercises

The following examples are progression exercises to build your body's skills up to handle a Full Planche

Planche Lean

The Planche Lean is a basic pushup style form where you lay on the floor flat on your stomach, with your hands placed on the floor next to your hips.
You then push your body up like a pushup until fully extended.
You let your body lean forward, letting your scapular muscles activate which helps your body understand the type of balance it should focus on that will help in balancing during a full planche and other variations.

Frog Stand

The Frog stand requires you to follow up on the planche lean by bringing up each knee and locking it behind your elbow while keeping your body leaning slightly forward.

Tuck Planche

The Tuck Planche builds up upon the frog stand by removing the assisted balance from locking your knees and elbows and letting the knees hang between the elbows. This requires you to keep the same forward leaning form but activating your core to assist in keeping the form

Straddle Planche

The straddle planche is an advanced form of the planche, where you straighten out your back from the tuck planche position, activating all the core muscles, arm muscles and scapular muscles, requiring focus to maintain the balance and plenty of practise for the body to understand the form.
The straddle planche requires you to spread out your legs for a wider balance, making it slightly easier than the full planche.

Planche

The full planche is the final step, requiring you to no longer using assisted balance by bringing your legs together, having all the balance work doen by your focus on keeping all the muslces activated.
